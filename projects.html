Yellow Jacket Space Program

Flight Dynamics Team - Aerodynamics and Stability Subteam

As part of this subteam, I focus on ensuring that the club's rocket flies properly, making sure it's both statically and dynamically stable.

This is done by using RASAero II to model the rocket and size its 4 fins such that the rocket has a static margin of 1.3 wet, and 1.8 dry, and then running this model through our in-house MatLAB based trajectory simulator, which has 6 degrees of freedom and tells us whether the rocket will be stable or not in flight.

I personally worked on a mass vs. fin size analysis, where I determined how small shifts in the starting center of gravity would impact fin sizing as a whole, trying to see if we could use the same size fins for a small range of potential masses. This allowed us to inform the Structures team as to how accurately they needed to weigh the vehicle once the time came.

I also aided the Launch Structures team with designing an electrical equipment enclosure using SolidWorks.

So far, being on this team has given me knowledge on aerodynamic principles and on stability, and has given me experience in modeling and simulating complex events. As I spend more time with the team, I aim to learn even more about the intricacies of space flight, and to play a larger role in ensuring it can happen successfully.
